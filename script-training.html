üéØ The Opening (10-15 seconds)

Your opener determines 80% of your success. Be warm, non-threatening, and create curiosity.

Recommended Opener

"Hi, I'm [Your Name] from Emerald Solar.

Don't worry, I'm not here to take your money!

It's just that I think this house qualifies for free solar on the roof - without paying any money out.

It's with one of the new schemes. I just need to check if you qualify..."

üí° Pro Tip: Say "free solar installation" NOT "free solar panels" - it sounds less salesy and more like a government scheme.
üö´ What NOT to Say
  • "I'm selling solar panels" - sounds like a sales pitch
  • "Can I have 5 minutes of your time?" - they'll say no
  • "Are you the homeowner?" as your first question - too abrupt
  • Mentioning monthly costs upfront - let them get interested first
üòä Body Language & Tone
  • Step back after knocking - don't crowd the door
  • Smile - even if nervous, it shows in your voice
  • Hands visible - don't have them in pockets
  • Clipboard/tablet visible - shows you're official
  • Relaxed pace - don't rush, it creates suspicion
  • Eye contact - but not intense staring

These questions determine if they're eligible. Ask all three before going further.

1 Do you own this property?
Why: Renters cannot sign up - the homeowner must agree. If NO ‚Üí politely end: "No worries, the scheme is only for homeowners. Have a lovely day!"
2 Is there more than one person over 18 in the household?

3 How much do you pay for electricity each month?
Why: Ideally ¬£80+/month. Higher bills = bigger savings = more motivation to sign up. If they don't know exact amount, estimate is fine.

üéØ Closing for the Appointment

After qualifying, your job is to book an appointment for the technical supervisor. This is NOT the final sale.

Transition to Close

"Great news - based on what you've told me, you should definitely qualify!

Our technical supervisor is coming round to a few neighbours' houses in the area. I can see if we can fit you in too.

They'll design the system for your roof, show you exactly how much you'll save, and go through all the numbers. Takes about 30-45 minutes.

Would a morning or afternoon work better for you?"

üí° Key phrase: "fitting you in" - makes it sound exclusive and in-demand, not like you're desperately booking appointments.

üí¨ Handling Objections

Tap each objection to see the recommended response.

"I'm not interested"

This is usually a reflex. Don't give up immediately.

"No problem at all - I completely understand. Just out of curiosity, is that because you've looked into solar before, or...?"

This often reveals the real objection (cost, hassle, bad experience, etc.) which you can then address.
"We're eating dinner" / "Bad time"
"So sorry to disturb you! I'll be super quick - literally 30 seconds. Do you own the property? [If yes] And you're on electric/oil heating? [If yes] Perfect - you could save ¬£200+ a month. Can I call you tomorrow to explain properly?"

Get their number for a callback - still counts as a lead!
"What's the catch?"
"Fair question! Here's how it works: Hometree Finance pays for everything upfront - the panels, batteries, installation. You pay a fixed monthly amount that goes up just 2.5% a year - less than inflation. The system is fully maintained, any repairs are free. Your savings should be more than the monthly cost. Our technical supervisor will show you the exact numbers for your house."
"I need to speak to my partner"
"Absolutely - that's exactly why we need both of you there when our supervisor visits. They'll go through everything together with both of you. What time are you both usually home?"

Turn this into an advantage - use it to book when both are in!
"I don't want to give my phone number"

This is common. Follow these steps:

Step 1 - Understand why:
"I completely understand - can I ask what's making you hesitant?"

Step 2 - Re-explain the process:
"The number is just so our supervisor can confirm the appointment time. They won't call to sell you anything - you've already expressed interest, they just need to confirm and ask if you have any questions beforehand."

Step 3 - It's required:
"Unfortunately the system won't let me submit the form without a contact number. It's just for appointment confirmation."

Step 4 - Final attempt:
"If you'd prefer, you could call our office directly - but they'll just ask the same questions I have. It's much easier if I complete it now while we're talking."
"I've already got solar panels"
"Oh great! Do you have a battery system as well? [If no] That's actually where a lot of savings come from now - storing energy to use in the evening when rates are highest, and selling back to the grid. We might be able to add batteries to your existing setup. Would that be worth looking into?"

If they have both, ask: "Know any neighbours who might be interested? We're fitting a few houses in this area."
"I'm too old for a 25-year lease"
"I hear that a lot, and it's a fair point. But here's the thing - the lease transfers with the property if you ever sell. It's actually a selling point because buyers inherit a lower electricity bill! And you start saving from day one. Our supervisor can show you exactly what the first year looks like."
"Send me some information"
"I can definitely leave a card, but honestly the information is all specific to your house - your roof, your usage, your savings. That's why our supervisor does a free home visit. It's much better than generic brochures. They can answer all your questions in person. What day works for you?"

Don't let them fob you off with "send info" - push gently for the appointment.
"I rent this property"
"Ah no problem - the scheme is just for homeowners. Do you happen to know if any of your neighbours own their places? Or do you have contact details for your landlord? They might be interested."

Always try to get a referral or landlord contact!
‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from trainees and customers.

What's the average electricity consumption in the UK?
UK average is about 2,700 kWh/year or roughly ¬£90-100/month at current rates. Devon rural areas are often higher (¬£100-150/month) due to electric heating in off-gas homes.
How much electricity does a solar panel generate?
Each 455W Jinko panel generates roughly 400-450 kWh/year in the UK. A standard 10-panel system produces about 4,000-4,500 kWh/year - enough to cover most of an average home's usage.
How much can the battery store?
Standard setup has 2x 5.8kWh batteries = 11.6kWh total. This can power an average home for about half a day to a full day depending on usage. It's designed to store daytime solar for evening use.
Can the battery last a whole year?
No - batteries store daily/weekly energy, not annual. They're charged daily by solar panels and discharged in the evening. Annual consumption is ~2,700kWh, battery stores ~12kWh - it cycles daily.
What does the monthly lease cost?
Typically ¬£80-150/month depending on system size. This is fixed and only rises 2.5%/year (less than inflation). The savings from reduced bills + selling energy back should exceed this cost.
How is it subsidized? When did the scheme start?
It's not a government subsidy - it's a Hometree Finance lease model. They pay for installation, homeowner pays monthly. The "free" part means no upfront cost. Smart Export Guarantee (SEG) pays for energy exported to grid. Schemes like this became popular from 2020 onwards.
What are Levelise and Snugg?
Levelise: Optimizes when to use/store/sell energy for maximum savings. Snugg: Carbon credit scheme - homeowners get paid for the carbon they offset. Both are free extras that increase overall savings.
What if something breaks?
Everything is covered - panels, batteries, inverter, installation issues. No call-out charges, no parts costs, no labour costs. Full maintenance for the entire 25 years. Zero risk to the homeowner.
What's a G98 vs G99?
G98: Notification for systems under 3.68kW (typically under 14 panels) - quick approval. G99: Application for larger systems - can take up to 45 days for approval. Most residential systems are G98.
